WORLDWIDE ENERGY

GLOBAL ENERGY PRODUCTION

The largest energy consumers in the world are Norway, United States, Canada, Iceland and the wealthy nations in the middle east such as United Arab Emirates, Qatar, Oman and Saudi Arabia. These countries consume about 100 times more per average person than third world countries.

As of 2022, the largest global source of energy comes from oil followed by coal, gas and hydropower. An upsurge in coal consumption can first be noticed after the era of Industrial Revolution at 1840 as manufacturing processes went through a significant transition.

RENEWABLE ENERGY

Amongst renewable energy sources, Tidal energy holds the highest share in power generation at 50.14% with a contribution of 19448.16 TWh of energy. Geothermal and Solar PV has the lowest contribution for global renewable energy summing up to only 2.46% with a contribution of 955.91 TWh of energy since they are very expensive to set up.

China leads the market for renewable energy production of all three sources (Biofuel, Hydro, Solar PV). The anomaly that can be spotted in the listed countries is Russia, while being in top 10 contributors, it's share of renewable energy comes only from Hydro.

CORRELATION BETWEEN ENERGY CONSUMPTION & CARBON DIOXIDE EMISSION

Primary energy consumption and CO2 emissions show a positive correlation. Asia Pacific region can be seen as the largest contributors of carbon dioxide emissions and energy consumption while Africa as the lowest.

Note: Logarithmic scale has been used for both the axis

Since 2012, a drastic increase can be seen in levels of CO2 emissions. A large drop in emission can be seen at 2020 which can be speculated as being caused by the outbreak of Covid-19. In 2021, the levels rise back up again. From 2012 to 2021, global carbon dioxide emissions had increased by 1979 million tonnes.
